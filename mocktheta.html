Discoveries by Srinivas Ramanujan

Mock Theta Functions

Introduction

Mock theta functions, introduced by Ramanujan, are special mathematical functions that deviate from classical theta functions in their behaviors. They were pivotal in his number theory work and have captivated mathematicians for their unconventional properties. These functions mimic theta functions but exhibit distinct patterns, making them a subject of ongoing research and exploration.

Contributions

Combinatorics and Mathematical Logic

Mock theta functions have connections to combinatorial problems and mathematical logic. They have been used to study certain combinatorial objects, like integer partitions, and their properties. Additionally, they have implications in mathematical logic and the theory of modular forms, influencing various areas of discrete mathematics and theoretical computer science.

Number Theory and Partition Theory

Mock theta functions have been instrumental in advancing our understanding of number theory, particularly in the study of partition functions. These functions have shed light on various aspects of partition theory, which involves understanding ways to represent integers as sums of smaller integers. Mock theta functions provide insights into the intricate relationships between different partition functions and the distribution of integer partitions.

Mathematical Physics - Quantum Field Theory

Mock theta functions have been employed in the study of certain physical phenomena, particularly in the context of quantum field theory and statistical mechanics. In the theory of black holes, for instance, they have been linked to the behavior of certain types of black holes in string theory and their microstates. The connection between mock theta functions and physical systems highlights the deep interplay between mathematics and theoretical physics.
